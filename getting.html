Getting Started

TaskFlow is a lightweight but powerful To-do list app which runs on your desktop and is available for Windows. It stores your data within your local machine making it easier to access your tasks offline. Begin your journey with TaskFlow with these introductory videos

SETUP

Installation

  1. Download the TaskFlow installer.
  2. Once it is downloaded, run the installer (TaskFlow.exe). This will only take a minute.
  3. By default, TaskFlow is installed under C:\Users\{Username}\AppData\Local\Programs\TaskFlow.
Alternatively, you can also download a Zip archive, extract it, and run it.

GET STARTED

User interface

At its heart, TaskFlow is a To-Do List Application. Like many other To-Do List apps, it has an add task field above and the add button next to it. Also, an area to show completed tasks below.

Basic Layout

TaskFlow comes with a simple and intuitive layout that maximizes the space provided for adding tasks while leaving ample room to browse and access the full context of completed and uncompleted tasks. The UI is divided into 4 main areas:

  • Task Creation Zone - The designated space for generating new tasks. Within this area, you can effortlessly craft and insert new tasks into your list.
  • Uncompleted task panel - The focal point for overseeing and controlling your tasks. Here, you can view, modify, delete, and manage your tasks effectively, such as marking as complete.
  • Completed task panel - The focal point for overseeing and controlling your tasks. Here, you can view, modify, delete, and manage your tasks effectively, such as unmarking as complete.
  • Menu Center - The core location for accessing various options and functionalities. In this space, you can explore and select from a range of choices to tailor your experience, such as clearing all tasks and clearing all completed tasks.
